Hello World!

My name is Connor, and I’m a high school librarian working in Johannesburg, South Africa. I’m also a newly minted blogger, planning to post to this website at least biweekly.


You might be wondering, what is this blog all about? And how did I come to start writing it?


Quite simply, this blog is about information and how we interact with it. Growing up in Johannesburg, I was interested in things that felt closeby, like novels set in South Africa and my high school chess team. The world of technology felt centred far outside of my reality, and often entirely out of reach. For those who experienced the progressive decline of Internet accessibility in South African over the last decade, you know that my feelings were not without justification. For younger readers or those outside of the country, let me give some background. Starting around 2020, changes to the climate created extreme and unpredictable currents, regularly damaging the undersea cables which connect South Africa to the rest of the Internet. Additionally, endemic load shedding has hurt the capabilities of server centres across the country, compounding the issue. As such, data costs have risen and accessibility has gradually diminished.


But a few years ago, during my time at UCT, I became interested in the study of information. I took classes that expanded my access to the Internet and I was surprised to find how much South Africans were doing, even with limited resources. I was also amazed by how much things had changed, and the new ways people all over the world were making information accessible, digestible, and engaging to larger audiences. Those experiences inspired me to become a librarian, and now that I’ve settled in at my new job, I’d like to keep exploring with this blog.


You can check out new posts from the Home Page or under Blogs. If you want to engage in the more academic side of this website, look under Theory. To follow my web developed journey, click on Tech. This blog will be changing over time as I improve and add to it, so some things may need to be archived, and you can find those things under Archive. To get in touch, click Contact.



Happy Web Surfing!
